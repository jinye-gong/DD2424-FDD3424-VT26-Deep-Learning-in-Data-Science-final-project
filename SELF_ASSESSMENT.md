## Evidence for the suggested grade

**1. We followed through on our proposal**

We compared VGG, depthwise separable, and ConvNeXt-style blocks on CIFAR-10 after aligning them to low, medium, and high FLOPs tiers. All planned training runs in our queue were completed (17 runs, 200 epochs each, seed 42). Our strongest result was **92.78% test accuracy** with `vgg_baseline` at width 64.

**2. The project goes beyond the basic requirement**

In addition to the E-level VGG baseline, we implemented two more architectures, built a shared training pipeline, and ran ablations on width, kernel size, and ConvNeXt depth. We also summarized the results with accuracy–FLOPs plots and simple design suggestions for different compute budgets.

**3. The report and code are organized for reproduction**

We wrote a structured report with related work, methods, results, and limitations. Training settings are in `configs/default.yaml`, results are logged in `logs/results.csv`, and figures are generated by script from those logs so the numbers stay consistent.

**4. We tried to interpret both helpful and unhelpful results**

For example, increasing kernel size often lowered accuracy on CIFAR-10, and widening VGG beyond width 64 did not always help. We discuss these patterns in the report and note that our conclusions apply to our setup (32×32 images, fixed training recipe), not to every possible dataset or training trick.

---

## Limitations

- We only tested on **CIFAR-10**, so the conclusions are mainly about small images.
- We used a **single seed (42)**, so small changes in absolute accuracy are possible with more runs.
- We report **FLOPs from thop**, not measured GPU speed or energy use.
- **MixUp and AMP were turned off** in the main study to keep the training recipe the same across models.
- One ConvNeXt depth run was interrupted early; we **retrained it to 200 epochs** before final reporting.

These limitations are discussed in our report. They also remind us what we would improve with more time (more seeds, more datasets, runtime measurements).
